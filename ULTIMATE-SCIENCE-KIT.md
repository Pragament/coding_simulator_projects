### ğŸ”¬ Introduction & Working Principle

**Introduction:**
Meet Oobleck - the magical substance that acts like a solid AND a liquid! Named after a Dr. Seuss book, this strange goo will blow your mind. When you poke it, it feels hard. When you let it sit, it flows like a liquid. Today we're making it EXTRA special with marbled colors!

**Working Principle:**
Oobleck is a **non-Newtonian fluid**. That's a fancy way of saying it doesn't follow the normal rules for liquids. Normally, liquids flow when you push them. But Oobleck is different - it's made of tiny cornstarch particles suspended in water. When you push on it (apply pressure), the particles lock together and act like a solid. When you stop pushing, they unlock and flow again!

---

| 5 | Add 2 drops YELLOW on another side | Color drop 2 |
| 6 | Add 2 drops BLUE on another side | Color drop 3 |
| 7 | Try spreading colors with stick | Watch what happens |
| 8 | Tilt cup slightly | Now watch THIS! |

#### How It Works:
Imagine the cornstarch particles are thousands of tiny dancers holding hands in water. When you're gentle (tilting the cup), they let go and flow past each other like a liquid. But when you push with the stirring stick (apply pressure), they grab hands tightly and become solid!

The colors create beautiful patterns because when you push, the solid oobleck resists mixing. When you tilt, the liquid oobleck flows and swirls the colors - creating a marbled effect!
