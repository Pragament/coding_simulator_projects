# 🚀 4-Week Deep Program

## Detailed Minute-by-Minute Lesson Plan

**Grade 6–8 | 2 Sessions per Week | 2–2.5 Hours Each**

Platforms used:

* **MakeCode micro:bit Simulator** – [https://makecode.microbit.org/](https://makecode.microbit.org/)
* **CreateAI micro:bit** – [https://createai.microbit.org/](https://createai.microbit.org/)
* **Tinkercad Circuits** – [https://www.tinkercad.com/learn/circuits/collection/circuits-basics](https://www.tinkercad.com/learn/circuits/collection/circuits-basics)

---

# 🗓 WEEK 1 — Foundations & Learning How to Learn

---

## ✅ Session 1 (2 Hours)

### Theme: How to Learn Online + First Code

### 0–10 min — Icebreaker

* Ask: “What is hard in math?”
* Ask: “Who thinks coding is scary?”
* Quick show of hands.

---

### 10–25 min — The Superpower: Learning Independently

Project on TV:
Open MakeCode.

Demonstrate:

1. Read instructions
2. Copy example
3. Change something
4. Break it
5. Fix it

Teach ChatGPT prompts:

* “Explain this block simply.”
* “Why is my score not updating?”
* “Give example using variables.”

---

### 25–45 min — Guided Practice

Students:

* Open MakeCode
* Build “Show Hello on Button A”
* Modify message
* Add variable counter

Teacher rule:
Do NOT touch their keyboard.
Guide verbally only.

---

### 45–60 min — Mini Challenge

Students add:

* Score variable
* Increase score when button pressed

Quick peer help allowed.

---

### 60–70 min — Break

---

### 70–100 min — Coding Foundations Intro

Teach via small demos:

* Variables
* Events
* Input / Output
* If–Else
* Loops
* Random numbers

Students build:
Number Guess Game (basic version)

---

### 100–120 min — Reflection

Students write:

* One bug I solved
* One thing I learned alone
* One math topic I struggle with

Homework:
Ask ChatGPT to explain one weak math topic.

---

## ✅ Session 2 (2 Hours)

### Theme: Core Programming Mastery

---

### 0–10 min — Warmup Game

Quick challenge:
Display random number 1–10.

---

### 10–30 min — Deep Concepts

Teach with micro demos:

* Modulus operator (%)
* Comparison operators
* Boolean logic
* Counters
* Nested conditions
* Functions

---

### 30–75 min — Guided Build

Upgrade Number Game:

Add:

* Lives ❤️
* Score counter
* Win/Lose screen

---

### 75–85 min — Break

---

### 85–115 min — Feature Menu

Students must add 2:

* Timer ⏳
* Difficulty mode
* Streak bonus
* Sudden death
* Hidden Easter egg 🎉

Teacher circulates and asks:
“What variable controls this?”

---

### 115–120 min — Exit Ticket

Students explain:
“What does IF mean?”

---

# 🗓 WEEK 2 — Math Game Builder

---

## ✅ Session 3 (2 Hours)

### Theme: Math Diagnosis + Game Design

---

### 0–15 min — Math Reflection

Students list:
3 weak math topics.

Use ChatGPT to revise.

---

### 15–30 min — Choose One Main Topic

Examples:

* Fractions
* Place value
* Percentages
* LCM/HCF
* Ratios

---

### 30–50 min — Game Design Sheet

Students answer:

* What math skill?
* How generate random question?
* How check answer?
* How score?

---

### 50–60 min — Break

---

### 60–110 min — Build Phase 1

Students build:
Core math logic.

Must include:

* Random question generator
* Correct/incorrect check
* Score counter

Teacher only asks guiding questions.

---

### 110–120 min — Demo Pair Share

Students explain game to partner.

---

## ✅ Session 4 (2–2.5 Hours)

### Theme: Gamification Upgrade

---

### 0–20 min — Show Advanced Features Demo

Show sample game with:

* Timer
* Levels
* Lives
* Bonus

---

### 20–90 min — Build Phase 2

Students must add:

Required:

* Win/Lose condition
* 1 difficulty level

Optional 2–3:

* Leaderboard
* Streak bonus
* Time bonus
* Speed round
* Combo bonus
* Penalty system

---

### 90–100 min — Break

---

### 100–140 min — Debugging Lab

Students:

* Test partner’s game
* Report 1 bug
* Fix 1 bug

Teacher enforces:
No teacher debugging unless stuck 5+ mins.

---

# 🗓 WEEK 3 — AI & Machine Learning

---

## ✅ Session 5 (2 Hours)

### Theme: AI vs Logic

---

### 0–20 min — Concept Lesson

On board:

Traditional Logic:

```
number % 2 == 0
```

AI:
Learns from examples.

Explain:

* Data
* Training
* Model
* Prediction
* Accuracy

---

### 20–40 min — Discussion

Ask:
“Why might AI make mistakes?”

---

### 40–60 min — Demo CreateAI

Open:
[https://createai.microbit.org/](https://createai.microbit.org/)

Show:
Create 2 classes:
Even & Odd.

---

### 60–70 min — Break

---

### 70–115 min — Student Build

Students:
Train only:
0 (Even)
1 (Odd)

Draw 20+ examples each.

Train model.
Test with new drawings.

---

### 115–120 min — Reflection

“What is AI good at?”

---

## ✅ Session 6 (2 Hours)

### Theme: Improve & Explain AI

---

### 0–20 min — Improve Model

Add messy drawings.
Test accuracy.

---

### 20–50 min — Connect AI to micro:bit

Show how prediction triggers:

* LED pattern
* Sound

---

### 50–60 min — Break

---

### 60–110 min — AI Presentation Prep

Students prepare explanation:

* What is training?
* What is prediction?
* Why mistakes happen?

---

### 110–120 min — Mini Presentations

---

# 🗓 WEEK 4 — Circuits, Robotics & Showcase

---

## ✅ Session 7 (2 Hours)

### Theme: Circuits & Logic

---

### 0–20 min — Basic Electronics

Explain:

* Current
* LED
* Resistor
* Button

---

### 20–40 min — Tinkercad Demo

Build:
LED + Button.

---

### 40–60 min — Student Build

Students replicate.

---

### 60–70 min — Break

---

### 70–110 min — Logic Extension

Add:

* Traffic light
* Blink patterns
* Button condition

Relate to:
If–Else
Loop

---

### 110–120 min — Quick Quiz

“What is input?”
“What is output?”

---

## ✅ Session 8 (2–2.5 Hours)

### Theme: Final Showcase

---

### 0–30 min — Final Polishing

Students fix bugs.

---

### 30–90 min — Presentation Round

Each student explains:

1. Math challenge
2. Game logic
3. Coding concepts used
4. AI classifier
5. Circuit logic
6. One bug fixed

Audience claps after each.

---

### 90–110 min — Reflection Circle

Ask:

* “What are you proud of?”
* “Can you learn alone now?”

---

### 110–120 min — Certificates + Celebration 🎉

Optional:
Hidden Easter Egg demo round.

---

# 🎯 Final Skill Outcomes

Students can:

* Use variables, loops, conditions confidently
* Build gamified math systems
* Train simple AI models
* Understand circuits as logic systems
* Learn from online tutorials independently
* Ask ChatGPT structured questions
* Present with clarity & confidence
